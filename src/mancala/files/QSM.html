For these examples, we’ll use the rules for the version of Mancala known as Wari. The board in Wari consists of two rows with six holes each. Here is what the board looks like.

Page 3

Wari begins with four seeds in each hole. On your turn, you can move the seeds from one of your holes by clicking on it.

Back Next

Page 4

The seeds move counterclockwise. If the hole you move seeds from had twelve or more seeds in it, that hole gets skipped when redistributing seeds.

Back Next

Page 5

If the last seed of a move lands in an opponent’s hole and results in two or three seeds in that hole, those seeds are captured. Additionally, the seeds in an unbroken sequence of holes containing two or three seeds immediately preceding the captured hole are also captured. In this instance, six seeds were moved from the fifth hole in the bottom row, so the seeds in the second, third, and fourth holes in the top row get captured.

Back Next

Page 6

If all your opponent’s holes are empty, you must make a move that moves seeds into them if possible. If you are unable to do so, the game is over and you capture all remaining seeds on your side of the board. In this instance, you must move the seeds in the last hole on your side of the board.

Back Next

Page 7

If you make a move where you capture every seed on your opponent’s side of the board, you also capture every seed on your side and the game ends.

Back Next

Page 8

When you or your opponent captures at least 25 seeds, that player wins and the game ends. If both of you have the same number of seeds and any remaining seeds are circling with neither of you able to make any captures, the game ends in a tie.
